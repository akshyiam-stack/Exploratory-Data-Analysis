# Global Sports Footwear Sales Analysis (2018–2026)

## Project Overview

This project focuses on performing an in-depth Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a global sports footwear sales dataset covering the years 2018 to 2026. The analysis uncovers important business insights related to revenue trends, brand performance, customer behavior, sales channels, product categories, and country-wise sales distribution.

Using Python libraries such as Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, the project demonstrates how retail sales data can be transformed into meaningful business intelligence through data cleaning, visualization, and statistical analysis.

---

# Objectives

- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing
- Analyze yearly and monthly revenue trends
- Identify top-performing brands and product categories
- Compare country-wise sales performance
- Study customer purchasing behavior
- Visualize business insights using charts and graphs
- Practice real-world Exploratory Data Analysis workflow

# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

The project includes extensive data visualization and business analysis to understand sales patterns and customer behavior.

## Revenue Distribution Analysis

Revenue distribution analysis was performed to understand transaction-level sales patterns. The analysis showed that most sales fall within a mid-range revenue segment, while a few high-value transactions act as outliers.

---

## Year-wise Revenue Analysis

Year-wise analysis was conducted to identify long-term business performance trends. Revenue remained relatively stable across the years with minor fluctuations, indicating consistent market demand.

---

## Country-wise Sales Analysis

Country-level analysis helped compare revenue generated across different international markets. Some countries contributed significantly higher revenue, making the dataset useful for regional business strategy analysis.

---

## Brand Performance Analysis

The project analyzed the revenue contribution of major sports footwear brands. The analysis revealed competitive market conditions where multiple brands contributed almost equally to total revenue.

---

## Category-wise Sales Analysis

Different footwear categories such as basketball, lifestyle, running, and training were compared to identify customer preferences. Lifestyle and basketball categories showed strong market demand.

---

## Price vs Revenue Relationship

The relationship between product pricing and revenue was studied using regression analysis. Results indicated that higher-priced products generally generated higher revenue.

---

## Outlier Detection

Outlier analysis was performed to identify unusually high or low revenue transactions. This helps detect business anomalies and understand exceptional sales behavior.

---

## Monthly Revenue Trends

Monthly sales analysis revealed fluctuations in purchasing behavior across different months, indicating possible seasonal demand patterns.

---

## Gender-wise Revenue Analysis

Revenue contribution across different gender categories was compared to understand customer segmentation and purchasing patterns.

---

## Multi-variable Analysis

Combined analysis of countries, years, and categories provided deeper insights into regional sales trends and changing customer demand over time.

# Key Business Insights

## Strong Product Demand

## Customer Purchasing Patterns

Higher product prices were positively associated with increased revenue generation.
